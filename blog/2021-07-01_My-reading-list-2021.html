Statistics

A genome-wide almanac of co-essential modules assigns function to uncharacterized genes
by Michael Wainberg, Roarke A. Kamber, Akshay Balsubramani, ..., Anshul Kundaje, and Michael C. Bassik
bioRxiv, 2019. DOI: https://doi.org/10.1101/827071.

I think a lot about standard omics analysis and statistical procedures used to reduce false positives. However, a lot of these methods assume independent measurements. But are genes, proteins, and metabolites really independent? This paper gives a thoughtful critique of current approaches and provides an alternative method for handling p-value inflation from the nature of these data using coessentiality networks.

Methods

Metabolomics and Isotope Tracing
by Cholsoon Jang, Li Chen, and Joshua D. Rabinowitz
Cell, 2018. DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2018.03.055.

As part of developing analytical approaches for Metaboverse, I have been thinking more about flux metabolomics and needed a good primer on where the technology is at. I read this review, which was masterfully written, and most importantly, very easy to read, even for a novice to the field like me. Highly recommend!

Ultra-fast proteomics with Scanning SWATH
by Christoph B. Messner, Vadim Demichev, ..., and Markus Ralser
Nature Biotechnology, 2021. DOI: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41587-021-00860-4.

I was fortunate to hear Markus speak "in person" at the virtual Keystone Symposia: Metabolic Decisions in Development and Disease, right around the time this paper was published. This technology has the potential to dramatically improve the throughput and speed at which we can capture proteomics data. I also recommend anyone interested in metabolism to keep an eye on Markus' lab. They are doing a lot of amazing systems metabolism research that has shaped the way I think about metabolism.



Statistics

Combining p-values via averaging
by Vladimir Vovk and Ruodu Wangy
arXiv, 2019. DOI: https://arxiv.org/abs/1212.4966.

I came across this paper reading up on methods for utilizing an aggregate p-value. The authors give a really thorough overview of several existing methods and some of the considerations for each. This paper was very informative while trying to decide the most appropriate approach for my problem. Personally, there was a lot of statistics jargon that I am just not used to since I am not a trained statistician, but I was still able to comprehend the important information that was relevant to me.



Job Market

A survey-based analysis of the academic job market
by Jason D Fernandes, Sarvenaz Sarabipour, ..., and Amanda Haage
eLife, 2020. DOI: https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.54097.

A previous post-doc in the Rutter lab, who is now running her own lab, shared this with us before she left. It was really fascinating to see some data-driven analyses of how the academic job market operates. A definite must-read for anyone getting ready to go on the academic job market!
