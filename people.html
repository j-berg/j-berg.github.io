Jordan Berg

I am a Ph.D candidate and NCI F99 Predoctoral Fellow in the Department of Biochemistry at the University of Utah

I work in the lab of Jared Rutter @ the University of Utah (Dept. of Biochemistry) and I am co-advised by Bei Wang @ the University of Utah (School of Computing). My primary focuses are developing computational tools and mathematical methods to aid in metabolism research and applying these and other methods to obtain novel biological insights into complex systems. Recently, I have been using these methods to explore the role of metabolism in cancer progression and heart failure. I also develop the bioinformatics software suites, Metaboverse, which aids researchers in contextualizing their metabolic data and automates the process of identifying regulatory events within their data; and XPRESSyourself, which adds to and automates the quantification and analysis of ribosome profiling and other gene expression data. My work is currently funded by a NCI F99 Predoctoral Fellowship (1F99CA253744).

Previously, I worked in the lab of Julianne Grose as an undergraduate isolating and phenotypically and genomically characterizing novel Erwinia and Brevibacillus bacteriophages.
